The desk in one paragraph

Six times a day, a team of software agents reads the market, pitches trade ideas to each other, argues about risk, and usually decides to do nothing. Every scan, pitch, rejection and trade is logged and scored. The desk trades a practice account because our own research (below) proved it hasn't yet earned real money — the honesty is the feature.

The agents & what each one hunts

Divergence Sniper Live

Hunts exhausted moves about to snap back.
  • Price makes a new extreme but momentum refuses to confirm it
  • Stretched 3+ ATR beyond its 50-period average
  • Sitting at a significant higher-timeframe level
  • Waits for a strong reversal candle before pitching
Best evidence of any scout: 52% of its winners ran past 2R in testing. Thresholds tightened to its proven slices.

Mean Reverter Live

Fades stretched rubber bands inside established ranges.
  • Range with 3+ touches of the same boundary
  • Price within 0.3 ATR of the edge, RSI at a deep extreme
  • Asia session only — ranges hold in Asia, break in London/NY
  • High-volatility ranges only; banks 60% profit fast at +1.2R
Every filter above was mined from its own trade history — the profitable slices survived, the rest were cut.

Trend Rider Benched

Hunted pullbacks in established trends.
  • Confirmed trend, pullback into the 20-period average
  • Rejection candle as the trigger
Fired on evidence: ~2,200 measured outcomes across 9 years, negative in every era and every condition. The pattern is too famous — the market taxes it. Still scans and learns; executes nothing.

Breakout Hunter Benched

Hunted coiled springs — compressed markets breaking out.
  • Volatility compressed to a fraction of normal
  • Price pressing a level touched 2+ times, enters on the break
Only profitable in the wild 2020–23 era; loses elsewhere by chasing tops. Benched until rebuilt to enter on the retest, not the pop.

Regime Engine Referee

Decides which scouts are allowed to hunt where.
  • Labels every pair TRENDING / RANGING / DEAD / NEWS with probabilities
  • Ranks currency strength across the whole board
  • Dislocation radar: stacks signals (compression, strength divergence, crowd extremes) — ⚡ on the Live tab
Wrong-regime pitches never happen: a range-fader can't pitch a trending market.

Risk Manager Hard rules

No discretion. Kills any pitch that breaks a rule.
  • Reward must be ≥ 2.5× the risk; spread ≤ 15% of the stop
  • No entries within 4h of high-impact news (auto calendar)
  • Crowd veto — never join a crowded position (the one rule that survived 9 years of testing)
  • Max 3 positions, 5 trades/week, no correlated doubles, 1–2% risk each, circuit breakers on losing streaks
In testing, ~85% of all pitches die here. That's the point.

Head Trader Selector

Ranks the survivors; picks at most two — or none.
  • Score = confidence × scout's track record × regime fit × macro alignment × session quality × room to run
  • Must clear an absolute bar; "no trade" is the default
Scout track records update weekly from their own logged results — hot scouts earn a louder voice.

Campaign Manager Trade care

Manages every position from fill to close.
  • Stop attached at the broker the instant of the fill — 24/7 protection
  • +1R: stop moves to breakeven (trade becomes free)
  • +2R: banks 40%, trails the rest to ride big moves
  • Strong trends: adds funded purely by locked-in profit
Designed to make losers small and constant, winners occasionally huge.

Fix Fader Verified edge · paper

Trades one hour a month-end: fades the London 4pm fix when rebalancing flow overshoots.
  • Last business day only; triggers when the fix-hour move is in its top 10% (rolling 250 days, per pair)
  • Enters against the move at the fix close, exits 60 minutes later — 12 pairs, tiny fixed size, catastrophe stop
  • Verified on 5 independent samples 2010–2026 (+0.18 to +0.35 pseudo-R); riding the same move loses in all 5
Month-end pension/index rebalancing is forced through one benchmark window — the push partially snaps back. The first edge to survive every control; earning live capital on paper.

Claude Scout On trial · off

The one discretionary seat: an AI pitching judgement calls, not patterns.
  • Each thesis is written to a public, append-only log and committed before its window opens — the timestamp is the proof it wasn't written with hindsight
  • No exemptions: same 2.5× reward bar, crowd veto, news blackout, correlation limits and 5-trade weekly budget as every scout
  • Every pitch is mirrored with the direction flipped. If flipping the calls does as well, the calls were noise
  • Verdict at 30 closed trades, on rules fixed in advance. Passing on a trade is logged too — declining is a result
It cannot be backtested: any "historical" AI thesis is written by a model that already knows what happened. So it is tested the only honest way — forward, on paper, in public. Currently switched off; a trial not yet started.

What 9 years of testing uncovered

FindingEvidenceConsequence
Chart patterns have no edge ~2,200 outcomes, 3 eras — continuation entries negative everywhere Trend Rider & Breakout Hunter benched
Edges rotate with eras Breakouts +0.22R in 2020–23, negative in calm eras Never trust a single-era backtest
Joining crowds is taxed −0.3 to −0.8R per trade, replicated in all 3 eras Crowd veto shipped — the desk's one verified law
Fading crowds ≈ breakeven Best fade slice: ~0R after costs A shield, not a sword — edge hunt continues
Our methods can fool us Null-control produced a convincing fake pattern, then failed to replicate Nothing ships without surviving unseen data
News is priced before you can click 526 surprise events: placebo "moves" matched real ones at 2h+ No news-chasing; blackout logic stays
Era-local patterns are real but die 7 statistically-solid fit-era patterns, 0 survived new eras Triple-era gauntlet is mandatory forever
Month-end fix fade WORKS Positive in 5 independent samples 2010–2026; momentum null negative in all 5 Fix Fader shipped to paper — first verified sword
Fighting the interest-rate gap looks costly — but we can't prove it Trades against a rate differential lost more in all 3 eras (−0.79R, −0.76R, −0.06R), yet the key era produced only 5 such trades and the confidence interval missed excluding zero by 0.008R Carry tiebreaker written, then switched off. The rules for passing were fixed before the test; it didn't pass. Lowering the bar afterwards is how research lies to you
A patient desk starves its own experiments ~15 trades a year means a sub-group question gets ~5 examples per era — too few to answer, no matter how real the effect Future sub-group tests wait for live data to accumulate, rather than being decided on thin samples

Bottom line: after ten pre-registered trials across nine signal families, one structural edge survived every control — the month-end London fix fade — plus one verified law (the crowd veto). Nine ideas were killed, including one that looked right in all three eras and still failed its own test. An eleventh trial is registered and not yet started: the Claude Scout arena. Everything trades on paper only until sustained results earn live capital.
